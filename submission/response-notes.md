# Response Notes for Previous Rejection Feedback

## Purpose
This file documents how the new RA-MARS manuscript addresses the key weaknesses identified in the previous Defence Technology rejection.

## Previous Editorial Concerns

The previous rejection indicated the following concerns:

1. Lack of sufficient novelty
2. Literature survey was too limited
3. Only 6 references were included
4. Manuscript did not demonstrate enough journal-quality depth
5. Defence Technology receives many submissions and prioritizes high-impact, original defence science contributions

## How RA-MARS Addresses the Novelty Concern

The RA-MARS manuscript will be positioned as an integrated defence mission-assurance framework rather than a generic UAV security or AI framework.

### Previous Weakness
The prior manuscript may have appeared too broad, conceptual, or insufficiently differentiated from existing work.

### RA-MARS Improvement
RA-MARS focuses on a specific defence problem: maintaining secure multi-UAV surveillance under jamming, GPS spoofing, and mission-data tampering attacks.

The novelty is based on integrating:
- AI-based attack detection
- Mission-risk scoring
- Adaptive mission-continuation logic
- Tamper-resistant mission logging
- Multi-scenario simulation-based evaluation

## How RA-MARS Addresses the Literature Survey Concern

### Previous Weakness
The previous manuscript included only 6 references, which is not sufficient for a journal-quality paper.

### RA-MARS Improvement
The revised research plan targets 45–70 references, with at least 25 references from 2021–2026.

The literature review will cover:
- UAV defence surveillance
- Multi-UAV mission planning
- Mission assurance
- RF jamming detection
- GPS spoofing detection
- AI-based anomaly detection
- UAV cybersecurity
- Blockchain or tamper-resistant logging
- Resilient autonomous defence systems

## How RA-MARS Addresses Defence Technology Scope

RA-MARS is aligned with Defence Technology because it focuses on:
- Defence surveillance
- Multi-UAV systems
- Contested environments
- Cyber-electromagnetic threats
- Mission resilience
- Secure autonomous systems
- Defence mission assurance

The manuscript should avoid being framed as:
- A generic AI paper
- A generic blockchain paper
- A quantum concept paper
- A broad UAV overview
- A purely theoretical framework without measurable validation

Instead, it should be framed as:
- A defence engineering paper
- A mission-assurance framework
- A simulation-based evaluation study
- A resilience-focused multi-UAV security contribution

## Safe Editorial Positioning Statement

RA-MARS is presented as a simulation-based defence mission-assurance framework for secure multi-UAV surveillance in contested environments. The framework integrates attack detection, mission-risk scoring, adaptive mission continuation, and tamper-resistant logging to evaluate UAV resilience through mission success, communication reliability, operational overhead, and data trustworthiness.
